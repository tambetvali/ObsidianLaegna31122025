### **The Absolute Differentiation of Karma**

**Absolute differentiation** refers to the concept of change in a system without reference to any other variables. It is a process where the outcome of a given action or intention is defined purely by its own internal characteristics, independent of any external systems. When we apply this to karma, it suggests that each action has an intrinsic nature that leads to a direct outcome, regardless of surrounding conditions.

From a **thermodynamic** perspective, we can think of absolute differentiation as akin to the principle of **entropy**. In thermodynamics, entropy is a measure of disorder in a system; it tends to increase over time. Karma, when viewed through the lens of absolute differentiation, follows a similar principle: actions, once performed, create ripples of consequences that spread outwards, causing the system to move from one state to another, potentially increasing the overall disorder or "entropy" in the karmic cycle.

For example, if someone acts out of malice or ignorance, the karma generated by that act will increase the "entropy" in the system. This negative energy disperses, creating a chain of consequences that cannot be undone, just as energy in a thermodynamic system cannot be completely reversed. However, these consequences can be managed by introducing order into the system, such as through mindfulness or deliberate action.

The principle of **absolute differentiation** shows that each individual action—whether good or bad—produces its own set of consequences. It can be likened to a closed system in physics, where the internal properties and actions of the system determine its future state. This approach highlights how karma is inherently self-contained, but can still interact with broader external systems, like society or the environment.
